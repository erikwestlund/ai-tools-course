# Matter-Of-Fact Language Skill

Use this guidance for written summaries, interpretations, captions, comments, and explanations in this repository.

## Style Rules

- Use direct, matter-of-fact language.
- Use bullets when listing steps, checks, findings, or limitations.
- Avoid cutesy, playful, or overly casual language.
- Do not use emojis.
- Do not speculate beyond the data or the code output.
- Avoid contrast templates such as "it's not X, it's Y" or "your job is not X, your job is Y."
- State uncertainty plainly when the data do not support a stronger claim.

## Interpretation Rules

- Keep interpretations close to the displayed summaries and plots.
- Describe associations or differences only when they are visible in the output.
- Do not infer causes, mechanisms, policy implications, or real-world prevalence from simulated data.
- If a variable meaning is unclear, say so and ask for clarification.
